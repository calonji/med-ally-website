# MedAlly Homepage Content Story

## Persona

MedAlly should speak to physicians, practice leaders, and clinical operators who feel the drag of documentation, coding cleanup, patient follow-up, and fragmented AI tools. The promise is not "more automation." The promise is a calmer clinical day with a physician-controlled path from encounter to signed chart.

## Message Thesis

MedAlly is the AI assistant for the work that follows the visit: documentation, clinical reasoning, treatment planning, communication, billing support, and specialty workflows in one reviewable context.

## Page Story

1. Hero: show a real clinician in a calm clinical setting. The page should immediately communicate trust, relief, and clinical seriousness.
2. Workflow: explain the core loop: listen, draft, review, code. The image should make the completed-chart idea tangible.
3. Scope: establish that MedAlly is broader than an ambient scribe. It covers documentation, decision support, revenue workflow, and physician-controlled automation.
4. Product proof: show concrete software moments: transcript-to-SOAP, evidence-backed differential review, and billing context tied to the note.
5. Sixteen-agent platform: explain the 16 agents as coordinated clinical workstreams, not as abstract AI novelty.
6. Proof and CTA: bring the story back to time saved, clinical confidence, and starting a physician-controlled workflow.

## Sixteen-Agent Story

The public page should group the 16 agents into categories a clinician can understand quickly:

- Documentation and multilingual capture: notes, summaries, translation, referrals, and follow-up instructions.
- Diagnostics and treatment support: differential diagnosis, medication safety, guideline checks, and care-plan suggestions.
- Operations and revenue workflows: coding, billing, scheduling, authorization, claims, and follow-up.
- Specialty adaptation and governance: specialty-specific workflows, audit trails, and physician approval.

Keep the story grounded in physician review. MedAlly can prepare work, connect context, and surface recommendations, but the clinician remains accountable for what enters the chart.
